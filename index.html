🔬 Project Overview

Key components of the Parkinson's Voice Analysis Pipeline

🎙️ Voice Biomarkers

Voice recordings capture subtle tremors, pitch instability, and noise-to-harmonic ratios that are hallmark signs of Parkinson's Disease — even before clinical diagnosis.

  • Fundamental frequency (Fo, Fhi, Flo)
  • Jitter: pitch variation measures
  • Shimmer: amplitude variation
  • HNR / NHR: noise ratios
  • Nonlinear dynamics (RPDE, DFA, PPE)

🤖 Machine Learning Pipeline

Seven state-of-the-art classifiers were trained, evaluated with 5-fold cross-validation, and compared across multiple performance metrics.

  • Support Vector Machine (RBF kernel)
  • Random Forest (200 trees)
  • XGBoost (gradient boosting)
  • Gradient Boosting Classifier
  • K-Nearest Neighbors
  • Logistic Regression & Decision Tree

📊 Dataset Details

The UCI Parkinson's dataset contains biomedical voice measurements from 31 people (23 with Parkinson's). Multiple recordings per person give 195 total samples.

  • 195 voice samples total
  • 147 Parkinson's (75.4%)
  • 48 Healthy controls (24.6%)
  • 22 acoustic features
  • Source: UCI ML Repository

🎯 How It Works

The prediction uses a Support Vector Machine with RBF kernel, trained on standardized voice features. Input values are scaled using the same StandardScaler fitted on training data, then classified by the decision boundary.

📏 Normal Value Ranges

  • MDVP:Fo: 100–260 Hz (typical speech)
  • Jitter(%): <1% (normal), >1% (abnormal)
  • Shimmer: <3.81 dB (normal threshold)
  • HNR: >20 dB (healthy voice quality)
  • PPE: lower values = more regular pitch

⚠️ Interpretation Guide

  • Probability >70% → High risk, consult doctor
  • Probability 50–70% → Borderline, monitor
  • Probability <30% → Low risk, likely healthy
  • Single test not diagnostic; repeat recommended
  • Context matters: age, medications affect voice
ℹ️ About This Project

Research Background & Methods

Understanding the science behind voice-based Parkinson's detection

🧠 About Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's Disease (PD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder affecting over 10 million people worldwide. It primarily affects dopamine-producing neurons in a specific area of the brain called substantia nigra.

Common symptoms include tremors, rigidity, bradykinesia (slow movement), and postural instability. Voice changes are among the earliest symptoms, appearing in ~90% of PD patients — often before motor symptoms.

🎙️ Why Voice Analysis?

Voice recording is a non-invasive, low-cost, and highly accessible biomarker. Parkinson's affects the laryngeal muscles, causing characteristic voice disorders called dysphonia.

Key voice changes in PD: reduced loudness, breathy/hoarse quality, pitch instability (jitter), amplitude instability (shimmer), and increased noise ratio (HNR↓, NHR↑).

📊 Dataset: UCI Parkinson's

Composed by Max Little (University of Oxford) in collaboration with the National Centre for Voice and Speech, Denver, Colorado. The dataset contains biomedical voice measurements from 31 people (23 with PD) using a microphone.

Citation: Little MA, et al. (2007). "Suitability of dysphonia measurements for telemonitoring of Parkinson's disease." IEEE Trans Biomed Eng.

🔬 Machine Learning Approach

We employed a supervised binary classification approach with extensive preprocessing (StandardScaler), feature analysis (ANOVA), and rigorous evaluation using stratified 5-fold cross-validation.

The best model (SVM with RBF kernel) achieved 94.87% accuracy and 96.67% F1-score, indicating excellent sensitivity for detecting Parkinson's cases.
